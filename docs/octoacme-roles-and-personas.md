# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing

### Role Summary
QA/Testing professionals ensure software quality by designing test strategies, writing test cases, and validating that features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Design test strategies and test plans
- Write and maintain automated and manual test cases
- Execute testing and report defects
- Validate acceptance criteria are met before release
- Collaborate with Developers and Product Managers on quality standards
- Participate in release validation and smoke testing

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal defects
- Reduce regression issues through comprehensive test coverage
- Improve testing efficiency through automation
- Provide timely feedback on quality risks

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- Bug reports and test case documentation
- Test results and quality metrics in weekly syncs
- Pre-release quality assessments

### Interactions with other roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on testability, review test coverage, and triage defects
- **Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria and user scenarios
- **Project Managers**: Report on testing progress and quality risks
- **DevOps Engineers**: Coordinate on CI/CD test automation and deployment validation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers are responsible for designing user interfaces and ensuring usability standards are met. They translate requirements into user-centric designs and ensure the product delivers an intuitive, accessible user experience.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Design user interfaces that meet accessibility standards
- Collaborate with Product Managers to translate requirements into designs
- Work with Developers to ensure design implementation fidelity
- Maintain design systems and UI component libraries
- Validate designs through user feedback and analytics

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and improve satisfaction
- Ensure design consistency across the product
- Balance user needs with technical constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- Prototypes and design specifications
- User research findings and usability test reports
- Design system documentation

### Interactions with other roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on understanding user needs and defining feature requirements
- **Developers**: Work closely on design implementation, provide assets and specifications
- **Business Analysts**: Use requirements analysis to inform design decisions
- **QA/Testing**: Partner on usability testing and accessibility validation
- **Customer Support Lead**: Gather feedback on user pain points to inform design improvements

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather business requirements, analyze stakeholder needs, and ensure alignment between technical solutions and business objectives. They bridge the gap between business stakeholders and technical teams.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements
- Analyze stakeholder needs and business processes
- Facilitate requirements workshops and user story mapping sessions
- Create functional specifications and process flows
- Validate that solutions meet business objectives
- Support change management and user acceptance testing
- Track and report on business metrics

### Goals
- Ensure technical solutions align with business needs
- Clarify requirements to reduce rework and ambiguity
- Improve business processes through analysis and optimization
- Enable data-driven decision making

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and user stories
- Process flow diagrams and functional specifications
- Stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Business metrics and analysis reports

### Interactions with other roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on requirements gathering and backlog prioritization
- **Project Managers**: Support planning with requirement analysis and effort estimation
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements and answer implementation questions
- **UX Designers**: Provide business context for design decisions
- **QA/Testing**: Define acceptance criteria and participate in UAT
- **Customer Support Lead**: Analyze support trends to identify business improvement opportunities

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure automation, deployment strategies, and site reliability. They enable efficient, reliable software delivery and maintain production system health.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code and automation
- Implement deployment strategies (blue-green, canary, rolling)
- Monitor system performance, reliability, and availability
- Manage security scanning and vulnerability remediation
- Coordinate release deployments
- Respond to production incidents and on-call rotation
- Optimize build times and deployment processes

### Goals
- Achieve fast, reliable, and safe deployments
- Maintain high system availability and performance
- Reduce manual deployment and infrastructure work
- Improve observability and incident response time

### Typical Communication
- Deployment notifications and status updates
- Infrastructure documentation and runbooks
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- Build and deployment metrics

### Interactions with other roles
- **Developers**: Support build/release processes, provide deployment tools and infrastructure
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate deployment schedules and communicate infrastructure risks
- **QA/Testing**: Collaborate on test automation in CI/CD and staging environment management
- **Customer Support Lead**: Coordinate on release communications and incident response
- **Product Managers**: Report on production metrics and system reliability
- **Business Analysts**: Provide technical context for infrastructure and deployment decisions

---

## Customer Support Lead

### Role Summary
Customer Support Lead acts as the primary contact for user feedback post-release. They coordinate with product and engineering teams to surface customer issues, identify trends, and drive support process improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Manage customer support team and escalations
- Collect and analyze customer feedback and support tickets
- Identify patterns and trends in customer issues
- Coordinate with Product and QA teams on bug reports
- Create and maintain support documentation and FAQs
- Communicate release changes to support team
- Ensure customer feedback loops into retrospectives
- Track customer satisfaction metrics (CSAT, NPS)

### Goals
- Deliver excellent customer support experiences
- Reduce time-to-resolution for customer issues
- Improve product quality through customer feedback
- Enable support team with knowledge and tools

### Typical Communication
- Support metrics and trend reports
- Customer feedback summaries
- Release readiness briefings for support team
- Escalations and critical issue notifications

### Interactions with other roles
- **Product Managers**: Share customer insights and feature requests to inform roadmap
- **Developers**: Report bugs with customer context and validate fixes
- **QA/Testing**: Coordinate on reproducing customer-reported issues
- **UX Designers**: Provide user feedback on usability and pain points
- **DevOps Engineers**: Collaborate on incident response and production issues
- **Project Managers**: Participate in retrospectives with customer feedback
- **Business Analysts**: Share support data to identify process improvement opportunities
